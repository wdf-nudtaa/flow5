\section{Introduction}
This documents summarizes the calculation method of the kinetic energy of a line vortex.

\section{Kinetic energy}
The velocity induced by a 2d vortex is 
$$\vec{V} = \frac{-\Gamma}{2\pi \,r} \vec{u}_\theta$$
The kinetic energy per unit distance of the fluid in a small surface element in the crossflow plane is
$$k = \frac{1}{2} \rho V^2 dS$$
Let 
$$dS = r \, dr \, d\theta$$
The kinetic energy in the crossflow plane is
$$K = \iint k \, dS = \iint \frac{1}{2} \rho V^2 \, 0.r \, dr \, d\theta$$
$$K = \iint \frac{1}{2} \rho \, \frac{\Gamma^2}{4\pi^2r^2} \, r \, dr \, d\theta=  \frac{\rho\Gamma^2}{8 \pi^2} \int \frac{2\pi}{r} dr$$
$$K = \frac{\rho\Gamma^2}{4 \pi} \int \frac{1}{r} dr$$
